Financial Trigger Finder

Enter a client's date of birth, hire date, planned (or actual) separation date, employee group, and pension formula, and get the dated list of financial triggers to review together: the RMSA credit-start ages, vesting, catch-up contributions, the age-55 retiree gate, the unreduced-pension service milestone, the Rule of 55, 59½, Social Security windows, the IRMAA lookback, Medicare enrollment, QCDs, and RMDs.

Section 01 · Interactive

Client inputs

Start with the employee group, pension formula, and employment status, then choose whether to enter exact dates or age / years of service, whichever the advisor has on hand. Either way the tool computes the same trigger dates (age- and service-based entries just read as "on or about").

Read this before relying on the output

This tool maps generic, dated planning triggers from statutory rules and the SPD-verified rules in the two plan guides. It assumes continuous service from hire to separation, uses exact dates where the plans count completed whole years, and cannot see breaks in service, rehires, disability provisions, the 2013 pension-choice election, or a client's actual pay. Indexed dollar figures are 2026 tax-year values. Always confirm a client's actual status at PG&E PensionConnect (mypensionconnect.com), Fidelity NetBenefits, the controlling SPD, and ssa.gov / medicare.gov before advising.

Separation checklist

The advisor's lens

The separation date is the most controllable variable on this page. A client who leaves before age 55 forfeits the RMSA and retiree medical entirely, even with a fully vested pension. A Final Pay / Final Average Pay client a few years short of the unreduced-pension milestone (30 years union / 35 management) is locking in a permanent monthly reduction. And a rollover before the Rule-of-55 year can cost penalty-free 401(k) access. Map the date against the timeline above before the client gives notice.

Section 04 · Reference

Age-trigger reference

The full trigger map behind the timeline. Statutory ages don't change yearly; the dollar figures are 2026 values, verify current-year figures before advising.

Age / eventTriggerWhat to review
45RMSA credits beginPG&E starts crediting the Retiree Medical Savings Account $5,000/yr the year the client turns 45. A spouse or registered domestic partner gets a parallel account. Employee-only bonuses ($1,000/yr past 15 yrs, up to $7,500 lump sum) are added at retirement.
46RMSA interest beginsThe account starts earning 4.5% annual compounding interest. The longer a client works past 46, the larger the medical-premium subsidy at retirement.
3 / 5 yrs serviceVestingCash Balance vests at 3 years; Final Pay / Final Average Pay at 5 years (or age 55 while employed). Separating unvested forfeits the pension.
50Catch-up contributions+$8,000 (2026) on top of the $24,500 402(g) limit, from the calendar year they turn 50. From 2026, clients who earned $150,000+ in the prior year must make catch-ups as Roth (now supported, see below).
30 yrs (union) / 35 yrs (mgmt)Unreduced Final Pay / Final Average Pay pensionWith this much credited service, the pension is fully unreduced at any retirement age 55–65. Below it, retiring before 65 applies the early-reduction table. Cash Balance has no reduction, so this milestone doesn't apply.
55Retiree gate · Rule of 55 · earliest pensionEnding employment at 55+ makes the client a PG&E retiree, the gate to RMSA and retiree medical (plus 10 yrs of Credited Service if hired on/after 1/1/2004). Also unlocks penalty-free 401(k) withdrawals (Rule of 55, forfeited by an IRA rollover) and the earliest Final Pay / Final Average Pay commencement.
59½Penalty ends everywhereThe 10% early-withdrawal penalty ends for 401(k)s and IRAs regardless of employment status.
60–63Super catch-up$11,250 (2026) replaces the standard catch-up for the calendar years the client turns 60 through 63; reverts at 64.
62Social Security earliest claimPermanently reduced (~30% below an FRA-67 benefit); earnings test applies if still working. ssa.gov
63IRMAA lookback beginsMedicare premiums at 65 are set by MAGI from two years prior, so the year they turn 63 is the first year Roth conversions and gains can raise their first Medicare premiums. 2026 tiers start above $109,000 single / $218,000 MFJ. medicare.gov
64¾Medicare Initial Enrollment Period7-month window opens 3 months before the 65th-birthday month. Missing it (without active employer coverage) means lifetime Part B penalties.
65Medicare · normal retirement · RMSA shiftUnreduced pension at 65 regardless of service. Medicare begins; the pre-65 bridge (retiree medical or COBRA) hands off. The RMSA subsidy shifts from 55% of premium (pre-Medicare) to 30% (Medicare). COBRA does not count as employer coverage for delaying Part B.
FRA 66–67Social Security Full Retirement AgeBy birth year: 1954 or earlier 66; 1955–59 66 + 2 mo/yr; 1960+ 67. Unreduced benefit; earnings test ends. ssa.gov
70Delayed credits max out~8%/yr delayed-retirement credits stop at 70. No benefit to waiting longer to claim Social Security.
70½QCDs availableQualified Charitable Distributions direct from IRAs (annual limit indexed); excluded from MAGI and count toward RMDs once those begin. irs.gov
73 / 75RMDs beginBorn 1951–1959: age 73. Born 1960 or later: age 75 (SECURE 2.0). First RMD may be delayed to April 1 of the following year (doubling two into one tax year). Roth IRAs and Roth 401(k)s are exempt. irs.gov

Plan-rule notes (not age-based)

  • Roth 401(k) source, effective Jan 1, 2026: the RSP now offers a Roth contribution option (in addition to pre-tax and regular after-tax). This is also what lets $150k+ earners comply with the mandatory-Roth catch-up rule.
  • Pension cash-out: a pension present value of $5,000 or less is paid as a lump sum regardless of formula (auto-rolled to an IRA if over $1,000, cashed out if $1,000 or less) unless the client acts within 60 days.
  • Excess Plan (high earners): pension amounts above the IRS §415 / §401(a)(17) limits ($360,000 of pay counted in 2026) are paid through a separate non-qualified, unsecured, not-PBGC-insured Excess Plan, as a lump sum at retirement. Most common for Management/A&T.

Born-1959 note: SECURE 2.0's text created ambiguity for 1959 births (73 vs 75); IRS proposed regulations resolve it to 73. Confirm final regs before relying on it for a 1959-born client. Group- and formula-specific pension rules (reduction tables, RMSA mechanics, match rates) live in the two plan guides in the sidebar.
